I'm currently an undergraduate Computer Science student at the University of Nottingham.

At the moment, I am most interested in software development and more specifically the optimisation of algorithms and programs. Thinks like time complexity, etc. That said, I always code a working program first, then look to make optimisations.

Hobbies

Guitars

I play a lot of guitar, mostly just songs that I like at this point. I played in a band a few years ago, however haven't got any creative projects going on in this regard at the moment.

I do repairs and modifications to all of my guitars myself. I own several, a lot of which I've customised to my liking. While my work isn't professional, I'd like to think that I give them a good life. My current favourite is my Epiphone Les Paul Custom:

As far as I know, this guitar is made of parts we bought as a kit from ebay. It was a birthday present that my dad built for me, and I haven't asked much about it. I did a lot more work on it myself recently, adding that stripe, and modifying the electronics. The pickup selector acts as a simple on/off, while one of the volume pots has been repurposed as a smooth control between head and bridge pickup.

Don't get too excited about the stripe. That's PVC tape. Like I said, not professional.

Music

Music has been one of my main passions for a long time. I mostly listen to rock, however also like a decent amount of EDM, jazz, blues, hip hop, etc. I'll probably enjoy any music you could play me, though artists that come off the top of my head include Foo Fighters, Green Day, Deftones, and Royal Blood.

I also have an intermediate knowledge of audio production, as well as live performance and recording. I managed an open-mic gig every half term at my sixth form college, using the audio equipment from the music technology department.

The experience was definitely helpful with my course at the time; I got the privilege to apply what I was learning in lessons to an actual practical situation, and often encountered issues where I had to adapt and improvise.

I also performed live at these shows every time too, doing a 15-minute set of whatever I wanted to play with my friends.
